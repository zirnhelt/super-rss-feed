# State of the Feed: Week of May 25–31, 2026

Hey everyone, quite a week in the feed! We ran 17 full aggregation cycles and pulled in nearly 16,000 articles total, with about 1,564 making the quality cut. AI and tech news absolutely dominated this week with 220 solid articles, while our homelab category pulled in a respectable 177 pieces—looks like people are genuinely excited about tinkering with their setups. News stayed steady with 846 articles, and we also picked up some interesting coverage across climate, science, and local stories. The wellness category was pretty quiet this week with just one article, so if you're looking for more health and lifestyle content, that might be something worth exploring.

We made some significant moves on the feed discovery front, adding a dozen new sources focused on Indigenous voices, agricultural innovation, and regional Canadian perspectives. You'll now see feeds from Nation Talk, the First Peoples Cultural Council, and several new Government of BC community programs covering everything from Guardian Programs to backcountry wildlife. We've also brought in agricultural voices like Western Producer and AgWeb, plus some really solid regional outlets like Pique and Adventure Journal. This should give our local and community-focused categories a lot more depth going forward.

On the technical side, we've got a handful of feeds with some hiccups—CBC Arts, Gizmodo, My Cariboo Now, My East Kootenay Now, and Civil Eats are all showing errors right now. Nothing permanent, but we're keeping an eye on them. Overall, the aggregator's running smoothly, and with these new feeds settling in, next week should get even more interesting. Let me know if you'd like to see different coverage areas!
