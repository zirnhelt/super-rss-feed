CONTENT TYPE PREFERENCES:

Strongly prefer:
- Long-form journalism with narrative depth and named sources
- Technical tutorials with real implementation steps, commands, or configs
- Community-impact stories showing how people or places changed
- Policy analysis with local-government or rural-service implications
- Scientist or researcher interviews explaining implications in plain language
- Case studies and project retrospectives ("what we learned building X")
- Academic or government report coverage (not the press release — the analysis)

Deprioritize:
- Press releases thinly disguised as news articles
- Listicles without substantive depth ("5 reasons why...")
- Social media reaction roundups ("Twitter responds to...")
- Conference or product announcements with no content yet
- Quarterly earnings and financial performance reports
- Pure opinion/hot-take without factual grounding or data — but NOTE: farm-trade-press analysis of working-lands economics (e.g. Western Producer opinion columns on equipment subscriptions, right-to-repair, input costs, commodity markets) is grounded industry commentary, not a hot-take, and should be scored on working-lands merit (see GEOGRAPHIC/PRIMARY INTERESTS above), not penalized as opinion
- Pure legislative milestone items with no substantive analysis: articles reporting only that "Bill X passed second reading" or "received royal assent" without explaining what the bill actually does, who it affects, or what changes. Score these 15-25 lower than you otherwise would. Exception: if the article explains the bill's provisions, its real-world effect on rural/BC communities, or provides meaningful analysis of the policy, score it normally on topic merit.
- Headline-only or very thin articles: if the body content is essentially just the headline restated with no additional facts, context, or reporting, score 15-25 points lower. The podcast generator notes when articles lack substantive body text; articles with genuine reporting depth are always preferred.

TIE-BREAKING AND EDGE CASES:

When an article sits on the boundary between two score ranges:
- Cariboo/local content: always round UP to next range
- Pure tech insider content with no community angle: round DOWN
- Health content without cited research: subtract 10 points from calculated score
- Syndicated wire story from outside BC: subtract 10 points
- Content explicitly bridging two SECONDARY interests: add 5 points
- Rural content from anywhere in Canada: add 5 points

When a story is covered by multiple sources:
- Prefer the source with the most original reporting and local context
- Williams Lake Tribune beats every other source for local stories
- Print sources beat broadcast sources for analytical depth

CATEGORY ASSIGNMENT NOTES:

local: Any article mentioning Williams Lake, Cariboo Regional District, 100 Mile House, Quesnel,
or surrounding Cariboo communities. Indigenous nation news in the region. SD27, CRD, WL council.

ai-tech: Artificial intelligence, machine learning, automation, robotics, developer tools,
software engineering, cybersecurity, cloud infrastructure, hardware innovation.

climate: Climate change science, renewable energy, clean tech, carbon policy, environmental
monitoring, wildfire ecology, water systems, biodiversity, conservation technology.
Do NOT assign to climate: stock market news, earnings reports, analyst ratings, or percentage
rallies even for clean-energy companies (those are financial news, not climate analysis).
Do NOT assign crime, missing persons, or RCMP/police stories to climate.

homelab: Self-hosted software, home networking, Raspberry Pi, NAS, home servers, privacy tools,
VPNs, network segmentation, home automation, 3D printing, maker projects.

science: Peer-reviewed research, scientific discoveries, space exploration, physics, biology,
medicine, materials science, earth sciences, academic findings with practical implications.
Do NOT assign to science: journalism industry news or obituaries of journalists even when
"journal" appears in the title. Do NOT assign product deals even when "paper" appears (e.g.,
"paper-like display 28% off"). Health/medicine studies with direct personal application
typically belong in wellness, not science.

scifi: Science fiction books, films, TV, podcasts, worldbuilding, speculative fiction, futures
studies, technology forecasting, solarpunk, cli-fi, hard SF with rigorous science.
Do NOT assign to scifi: promotional e-book deals or free Kindle offers even when "book" appears.
Do NOT assign general news (politics, crime, international affairs) to scifi even if the source
sometimes covers culture. An article must be genuinely about the SF/fantasy genre or its ideas.

wellness: Personal health, nutrition, mental health, fitness, and medicine — NOT sports, tech
reviews, or crime/conflict news. Do NOT assign to wellness: breaking news about violence, war,
active shooters, crime investigations, or RCMP/police incidents even when a health facility is
incidentally mentioned. "Alert for shooter at health clinic" is a crime/news story, not wellness.
"Iran war live" is geopolitics, not wellness.

news: General news that does not fit the above categories — politics, economy, social issues,
community events, national/international affairs relevant to rural British Columbia.
