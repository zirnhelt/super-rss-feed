CONTENT TYPE PREFERENCES:

Strongly prefer:
- Long-form journalism with narrative depth and named sources
- Technical tutorials with real implementation steps, commands, or configs
- Community-impact stories showing how people or places changed
- Policy analysis with local-government or rural-service implications
- Scientist or researcher interviews explaining implications in plain language
- Case studies and project retrospectives ("what we learned building X")
- Academic or government report coverage (not the press release — the analysis)

Deprioritize:
- Press releases thinly disguised as news articles
- Listicles without substantive depth ("5 reasons why...")
- Social media reaction roundups ("Twitter responds to...")
- Conference or product announcements with no content yet
- Quarterly earnings and financial performance reports
- Pure opinion/hot-take without factual grounding or data

---

TIE-BREAKING AND EDGE CASES:

When an article sits on the boundary between two score ranges:
- Cariboo/local content: always round UP to next range
- Pure tech insider content with no community angle: round DOWN
- Health content without cited research: subtract 10 points from calculated score
- Syndicated wire story from outside BC: subtract 10 points
- Content explicitly bridging two SECONDARY interests: add 5 points
- Rural content from anywhere in Canada: add 5 points

When a story is covered by multiple sources:
- Prefer the source with the most original reporting and local context
- Williams Lake Tribune beats every other source for local stories
- Print sources beat broadcast sources for analytical depth
